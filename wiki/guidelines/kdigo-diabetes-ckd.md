# KDIGO / ADA-KDIGO Diabetes in CKD

## Why This Matters
Diabetes kidney disease management is now layered therapy: screening, renin-angiotensin system blockade when indicated, SGLT2 inhibitor foundation, glycemic management, blood pressure/lipid control, and additional kidney/cardiovascular risk reduction when residual risk remains.

## Practical Domains

| Domain | Clinician Use |
|--------|---------------|
| Screening | Annual eGFR and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio in most patients with diabetes. |
| Foundation therapy | ACEi/ARB for albuminuria with hypertension; SGLT2 inhibitor for eligible CKD. |
| Glycemic therapy | Metformin when eGFR permits; GLP-1 RA with proven benefit when targets unmet or cardiorenal/weight benefit is needed. |
| Residual risk | Consider finerenone in appropriate albuminuric CKD despite ACEi/ARB, with potassium monitoring. |
| Referral | Nephrology for rapid eGFR decline, advanced CKD, severe albuminuria, resistant hypertension, or diagnostic uncertainty. |

## Clinical Takeaways

- **Albuminuria changes treatment:** Normal creatinine alone is not enough; urine ACR identifies high-risk patients earlier.
- **SGLT2 inhibitors are kidney drugs:** Their benefit is not simply from glucose lowering and extends into lower eGFR ranges depending on agent/label/guideline.
- **GLP-1 RA layering is now important:** FLOW strengthened semaglutide evidence in type 2 diabetes with CKD.
- **Safety is operational:** eGFR thresholds, volume status, genital infection counseling, perioperative holds, and ketoacidosis risk need explicit workflow.
