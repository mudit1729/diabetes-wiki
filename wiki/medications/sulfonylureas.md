# Sulfonylureas

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Cost-constrained type 2 diabetes needing A1C reduction | Low cost and strong glucose-lowering effect. |
| Short-term bridge when preferred agents are inaccessible | Can reduce glucose while access issues are solved. |

## Before Starting

- Estimate hypoglycemia risk: age, CKD, skipped meals, alcohol, driving/occupation risk, prior severe lows, living alone.
- Avoid long-acting/high-risk agents when safer alternatives are available.
- Set an exit plan if GLP-1 RA, SGLT2 inhibitor, or insulin becomes available.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| Hypoglycemia | Ask about sweating, tremor, confusion, falls, night symptoms. |
| Meal pattern | Risk rises with missed meals or fasting. |
| Kidney function | CKD increases hypoglycemia risk. |
| Weight | Expect possible gain. |

## De-Intensify First When

- A1C is at or below goal and lows occur.
- Insulin is added or intensified.
- GLP-1 RA/tirzepatide causes rapid improvement.
- CKD progresses or appetite falls.
