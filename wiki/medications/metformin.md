# Metformin

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| New type 2 diabetes without contraindication | Low cost, oral, durable experience, weight-neutral/modest loss. |
| Combination therapy foundation | Pairs with SGLT2 inhibitors, GLP-1 RA, DPP-4 inhibitors, insulin, and others. |
| Cost-constrained care | High value when outcome drugs are unaffordable. |

## Before Starting

- Check eGFR and acute illness risk.
- Ask about severe GI intolerance, alcohol use disorder, advanced liver disease, unstable HF/hypoxia, and prior lactic acidosis.
- Discuss slow titration and extended-release option if GI intolerance occurs.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| eGFR | Baseline and periodically; more often in CKD/older adults. |
| GI tolerance | Nausea, diarrhea, appetite loss. |
| B12 | Consider periodic testing, especially anemia, neuropathy, or long duration. |
| Acute illness | Hold when dehydration, sepsis, hypoxia, or contrast-risk scenario applies. |

## Do Not Use / Pause

- Avoid when eGFR is below label threshold or during high-risk acute illness.
- Hold around iodinated contrast when label/guideline criteria indicate.
- Do not continue through dehydration, hypoxia, sepsis, or shock.
