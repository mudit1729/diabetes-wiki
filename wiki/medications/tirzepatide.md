# Tirzepatide

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Type 2 diabetes with obesity and need for very high glycemic efficacy | Dual GIP/GLP-1 therapy has large A1C and weight effects. |
| Patient where avoiding insulin-associated weight gain matters | Useful before or alongside insulin simplification when appropriate. |
| Obesity-driven cardiometabolic risk | Weight effect may be clinically central, but outcome positioning should follow current CV/renal data and labels. |

## Before Starting

- Review thyroid cancer/MEN2 contraindication language, serious hypersensitivity history, pancreatitis concern, severe GI disease, gallbladder symptoms, pregnancy plans, and cost/access.
- Warn that delayed gastric emptying may affect tolerability and medication timing.
- If using insulin or sulfonylurea, proactively lower hypoglycemia risk.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| A1C / CGM | Confirm glycemic response and hypoglycemia after dose changes. |
| Weight / nutrition | Avoid excessive loss in frail patients. |
| GI tolerance | Escalate slowly and pause escalation when needed. |
| Hydration / kidney function | Reassess during vomiting or poor intake. |

## Do Not Use / Pause

- Do not use in patients with label contraindications such as personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2.
- Pause and evaluate for severe GI symptoms, dehydration, or pancreatitis concern.
- Do not use as insulin replacement in type 1 diabetes or insulin-deficient ketosis-prone states.
