# Semaglutide

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Type 2 diabetes with obesity or need for high A1C efficacy | Strong A1C and weight effect in the SUSTAIN program. |
| Type 2 diabetes with established ASCVD or high CV risk | SUSTAIN-6 supports MACE reduction. |
| Type 2 diabetes with CKD and albuminuric risk | FLOW supports kidney and cardiovascular death benefit. |
| Insulin/sulfonylurea-treated patient needing weight-friendly intensification | Can reduce insulin escalation, but hypoglycemia-prone drugs may need dose reduction. |

## Before Starting

- Ask about pancreatitis history, severe GI disease, gallbladder disease, pregnancy plans, and personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma or MEN2.
- Check eye history. Rapid A1C improvement can worsen existing retinopathy in some patients.
- Review insulin/sulfonylurea doses and reduce if hypoglycemia risk is high.
- Discuss cost, supply, injection technique, and expected GI effects.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| A1C / CGM | Reassess after titration interval. |
| Weight and appetite | Track benefit and excess weight loss/frailty risk. |
| GI tolerance | Nausea, vomiting, dehydration, constipation, reflux. |
| Retinopathy | Eye follow-up when rapid A1C drop or known retinopathy. |
| Kidney function | Especially if vomiting/dehydration occurs. |

## Do Not Use / Pause

- Avoid in patients with contraindicated thyroid cancer syndromes per label.
- Hold or reassess during severe vomiting, dehydration, bowel obstruction concern, or acute pancreatitis concern.
- Avoid using as a substitute for insulin in insulin-deficient states.
