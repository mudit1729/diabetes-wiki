# Basal Insulin

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Symptomatic hyperglycemia or catabolic features | Insulin is the fastest reliable glucose-lowering therapy. |
| Very high glucose despite non-insulin therapy | Basal insulin can stabilize fasting hyperglycemia. |
| Contraindication/intolerance/cost barrier to preferred non-insulin options | Insulin remains effective and widely available. |

## Before Starting

- Decide whether insulin deficiency, ketosis, infection, steroids, or pancreatogenic diabetes is present.
- Confirm patient can monitor glucose and treat hypoglycemia.
- Review sulfonylurea dose, meal reliability, renal function, visual/manual ability, and cost of analog vs human insulin.
- Prefer GLP-1 RA before insulin when appropriate and accessible.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| Fasting glucose | Main titration signal for basal insulin. |
| Overnight lows | Ask directly; consider CGM when feasible. |
| Weight | Track insulin-associated gain. |
| Injection technique | Needle length, rotation, storage, priming. |

## Do Not Do

- Do not keep raising basal insulin when fasting glucose is controlled but A1C remains high.
- Do not ignore recurrent lows because A1C is above goal.
- Do not use outpatient sliding-scale-only plans as chronic therapy.
