# Finerenone

## Best Fit

| Use Case | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Type 2 diabetes with albuminuric CKD despite ACEi/ARB | Adds nonsteroidal mineralocorticoid receptor antagonism for residual kidney/CV risk. |
| Patient already considered for layered kidney protection | Fits after BP/RAS blockade/SGLT2 foundation is addressed and potassium monitoring is feasible. |

## Before Starting

- Confirm albuminuric CKD phenotype and ACEi/ARB status when tolerated.
- Check eGFR and potassium before prescribing.
- Review hyperkalemia risks: advanced CKD, potassium supplements, salt substitutes, MRAs, ACEi/ARB dose changes, trimethoprim, NSAIDs, diet.
- Check drug interactions, especially CYP3A4 inhibitors/inducers per label.

## Monitoring

| Item | Practical Check |
|------|-----------------|
| Potassium | Baseline, about 4 weeks after start/dose change, then by risk. |
| eGFR | Baseline and periodically. |
| BP / volume | Watch hypotension in multi-drug regimens. |
| Medication changes | Recheck potassium after adding interacting or potassium-raising drugs. |

## Do Not Use / Pause

- Do not start if potassium is above label threshold.
- Hold or reduce for hyperkalemia per label protocol.
- Avoid strong CYP3A4 inhibitors and follow label interaction guidance.
