# Type 2 Diabetes With CKD

## First Pass

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Confirm CKD stage with eGFR and urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio. | Albuminuria identifies high-risk patients missed by creatinine alone. |
| 2 | Review BP, ACEi/ARB use, potassium, volume status, and NSAID exposure. | Kidney protection starts before glucose drug selection. |
| 3 | Start or continue metformin only if eGFR and tolerability allow. | Low-cost glycemic foundation; dose/stop by eGFR and acute illness risk. |
| 4 | Add an SGLT2 inhibitor when eligible. | Strong kidney and heart failure outcome evidence. |
| 5 | Add GLP-1 RA with outcome evidence when A1C/weight/ASCVD risk remains important or SGLT2 is not enough/tolerated. | FLOW supports semaglutide kidney outcome benefit in type 2 diabetes with CKD. |

## Practical Sequence

1. **Albuminuric CKD, eGFR eligible:** ACEi/ARB if indicated, then SGLT2 inhibitor unless contraindicated.
2. **Persistent A1C above goal or obesity/ASCVD risk:** Prefer GLP-1 RA with outcome evidence, especially semaglutide/liraglutide/dulaglutide depending on access.
3. **Persistent albuminuric risk despite standard therapy:** Consider finerenone when potassium/eGFR criteria fit and monitoring is feasible.
4. **Need insulin:** Use basal insulin carefully; reduce sulfonylurea exposure and hypoglycemia risk.
5. **Advanced CKD:** Avoid blind intensification. Recheck drug labels, renal dosing, nutrition, anemia, and nephrology involvement.

## Monitoring

| Item | Timing |
|------|--------|
| eGFR / creatinine | Baseline, after hemodynamic-risk changes, then every 3-6 months by risk |
| Urine ACR | At least annually; more often if high risk or changing therapy |
| Potassium | With ACEi/ARB, finerenone, advanced CKD, or intercurrent illness |
| Hypoglycemia | Every visit when insulin/sulfonylurea is used |
| Weight / GI tolerance | During GLP-1 RA titration |

## Avoid

- Treating a normal creatinine as "no kidney disease" without urine ACR.
- Stopping SGLT2 inhibitor only because A1C effect is small at lower eGFR.
- Adding GLP-1 RA without reducing insulin/sulfonylurea when hypoglycemia risk is high.
- Missing sick-day and perioperative holds for SGLT2 inhibitors.
