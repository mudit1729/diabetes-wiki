# Type 2 Diabetes With ASCVD or High ASCVD Risk

## First Pass

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Confirm ASCVD phenotype: prior MI, stroke, PAD, revascularization, or high-risk primary prevention. | Drug priority differs by established ASCVD vs risk factors only. |
| 2 | Treat lipids, BP, smoking, kidney disease, and obesity in parallel. | Glucose drugs do not replace complete risk-factor control. |
| 3 | Prefer GLP-1 RA with CVOT benefit when weight/ASCVD risk dominates. | LEADER, SUSTAIN-6, and REWIND support MACE reduction. |
| 4 | Prefer SGLT2 inhibitor when CKD or heart failure overlaps. | Strong heart failure and kidney evidence, with CV benefit in selected populations. |
| 5 | Individualize A1C target and avoid hypoglycemia. | ACCORD/ADVANCE/VADT caution against aggressive high-burden lowering in high-risk patients. |

## Practical Sequence

1. **Established ASCVD, obesity, no heart failure priority:** GLP-1 RA with CVOT benefit.
2. **ASCVD plus CKD or heart failure:** SGLT2 inhibitor first or early, then GLP-1 RA if A1C/weight/ASCVD risk remains.
3. **High-risk primary prevention:** Use shared decision-making; REWIND and DECLARE are useful context, but absolute benefit depends on baseline risk.
4. **Already on insulin/sulfonylurea:** Reduce hypoglycemia-prone therapy as GLP-1 RA/SGLT2 therapy is added and A1C improves.
5. **Cost barrier:** Optimize metformin, statin, BP, smoking, diet, activity, and safer low-cost glycemic choices while documenting why outcome drugs are not used.

## Do Not Miss

- High-intensity or maximally tolerated statin where indicated.
- BP target and ACEi/ARB when albuminuria/hypertension fit.
- Antiplatelet therapy only when appropriate for ASCVD context.
- PAD/foot assessment in high-risk patients.
