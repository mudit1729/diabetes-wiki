# Type 2 Diabetes With Heart Failure

## First Pass

| Step | Action | Why |
|------|--------|-----|
| 1 | Classify HF phenotype: HFrEF, HFmrEF, HFpEF, recent hospitalization, congestion status. | Treatment urgency and safety checks differ. |
| 2 | Add SGLT2 inhibitor when eligible. | Strong HF hospitalization benefit across diabetes and non-diabetes HF populations. |
| 3 | Avoid or de-emphasize agents that worsen fluid retention. | Pioglitazone is usually a poor fit in symptomatic HF. |
| 4 | Reassess insulin burden and hypoglycemia. | HF admissions, renal swings, and poor intake increase hypoglycemia risk. |
| 5 | Coordinate with HF guideline-directed therapy. | Diabetes treatment should not compete with beta blocker, ARNI/ACEi/ARB, MRA, and diuretic optimization. |

## Practical Sequence

1. **Stable outpatient HF:** Start SGLT2 inhibitor after checking eGFR, volume status, genital infection risk, and affordability.
2. **Recently decompensated HF:** Confirm hemodynamic stability and diuretic plan; counsel on volume symptoms.
3. **Obesity/ASCVD also present:** Add GLP-1 RA later for weight/ASCVD/glycemia if GI tolerance and cost are acceptable.
4. **Advanced CKD overlap:** Follow CKD algorithm and eGFR labeling.
5. **Insulin-treated patient:** Use CGM if feasible, simplify regimens after admissions, and avoid sliding-scale-only outpatient plans.

## Safety Checklist

- Sick-day guidance for SGLT2 inhibitor holds.
- Perioperative and fasting holds.
- Euglycemic DKA warning symptoms.
- Genital mycotic infection prevention and early treatment.
- Volume depletion review when diuretic dose changes.
