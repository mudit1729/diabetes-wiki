# Hypoglycemia Risk and De-Intensification

## Identify High-Risk Patients

| Risk Factor | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| Prior severe hypoglycemia | Strong predictor of recurrence. |
| Insulin or sulfonylurea use | Main medication drivers. |
| CKD or fluctuating intake | Drug clearance and carbohydrate intake become unpredictable. |
| Older age, cognitive impairment, living alone | Harm from an episode is higher. |
| Tight A1C with symptoms or CGM lows | A low A1C may represent overtreatment. |

## De-Intensification Workflow

1. Confirm the pattern: fasting, overnight, post-exercise, missed meal, alcohol, renal decline, or dosing error.
2. Relax the A1C/glucose goal when benefit is unlikely to exceed harm.
3. Reduce insulin or sulfonylurea first.
4. Substitute lower-hypoglycemia-risk agents when feasible: metformin, SGLT2 inhibitor, GLP-1 RA, DPP-4 inhibitor.
5. Use CGM when available for insulin-treated patients or recurrent unexplained lows.
6. Prescribe/teach rescue glucagon when severe hypoglycemia risk exists and access allows.

## Practical Adjustments

| Pattern | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Overnight lows | Reduce basal insulin; check dinner dose/snack/alcohol. |
| Pre-lunch lows | Review breakfast insulin/sulfonylurea and morning activity. |
| Lows with CKD progression | Recalculate dosing needs and stop high-risk oral agents where needed. |
| A1C <7% in frail older adult on insulin/SU | De-intensify unless there is a compelling reason not to. |
| Recurrent lows despite dose changes | Reassess diagnosis, adrenal/renal/liver disease, nutrition, and medication errors. |
