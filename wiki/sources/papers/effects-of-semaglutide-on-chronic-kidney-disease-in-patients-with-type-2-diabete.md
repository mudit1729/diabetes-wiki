# Effects of Semaglutide on Chronic Kidney Disease in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes

## Clinical Question

Does semaglutide reduce chronic kidney disease progression and cardiovascular death in patients with type 2 diabetes?

## PICO

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| **Population** | Adults with type 2 diabetes and chronic kidney disease |
| **Intervention** | Semaglutide 1.0 mg weekly |
| **Comparator** | Placebo |
| **Outcome** | Major kidney events and kidney-related or cardiovascular death |

## Key Results

- **Primary outcome:** Reduced by 24% with semaglutide (HR 0.76; 95% CI, 0.66-0.88; P=0.0003).
- **Composite kidney outcome:** HR 0.79; 95% CI, 0.66-0.94.
- **Cardiovascular death:** Reduced by 29% (HR 0.71; 95% CI, 0.56-0.89).
- **eGFR slope:** Improved by 1.16 ml/min/1.73 m² per year (P<0.001).
- **NNT:** 20 over 3 years for the primary outcome (95% CI, 14-40).

## What Changed?

- **Kidney protection:** Semaglutide now has outcome evidence for reducing CKD progression in type 2 diabetes with CKD.
- **Cardiovascular mortality:** Benefit extends beyond glucose and weight to reduced cardiovascular death.
- **Clinical positioning:** Semaglutide becomes a stronger option when kidney and cardiovascular risk reduction are treatment priorities.

## What Did Not Change?

- **Generalizability:** Effects in under-represented racial groups remain uncertain.
- **Combination therapy:** Added benefit with concurrent SGLT2 inhibitors or other kidney-protective therapies remains incompletely defined.

## How I Would Explain This to a Patient

Semaglutide is a once-weekly injection that can help protect your kidneys and heart if you have type 2 diabetes and kidney disease. In this study, people taking it had fewer serious kidney problems and fewer heart-related deaths. It does not replace your other kidney and diabetes medicines, but it may add extra protection.

- **Population relevance:** Relevant to Indian patients with type 2 diabetes and CKD, but local affordability and access will drive use.
