# SGLT2 CVOT Program

## Clinical Question
Do SGLT2 inhibitors improve cardiovascular, heart failure, and kidney outcomes in type 2 diabetes beyond glucose lowering?

## Anchor Trials

| Trial | Drug | Practical Point |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| EMPA-REG OUTCOME | Empagliflozin | Reduced MACE, cardiovascular death, heart failure hospitalization, and all-cause mortality in established ASCVD. |
| CANVAS Program | Canagliflozin | Reduced MACE; showed renal benefit signal, with safety attention to amputation/fracture concerns in context. |
| DECLARE-TIMI 58 | Dapagliflozin | Broad population; strongest signal for heart failure hospitalization and renal outcomes, including many without established ASCVD. |
| CREDENCE / DAPA-CKD / EMPA-KIDNEY | Canagliflozin / dapagliflozin / empagliflozin | Established SGLT2 inhibitors as kidney-protective therapy in CKD populations. |

## Clinical Takeaways

- **Heart failure and CKD first:** SGLT2 inhibitors are priority therapy in type 2 diabetes with heart failure or CKD when eligible.
- **Benefits are not just A1C:** Cardiorenal benefit persists even when glucose-lowering effect is modest at lower eGFR.
- **Primary prevention differs:** DECLARE is especially useful for patients with multiple risk factors but no established ASCVD.
- **Safety workflow matters:** Counsel on genital mycotic infection, volume depletion, sick-day holds, perioperative holds, and ketoacidosis risk.
