# GLP-1 CVOT Program

## Clinical Question
Do GLP-1 receptor agonists reduce cardiovascular events in type 2 diabetes, and how should clinicians choose them?

## Anchor Trials

| Trial | Drug | Practical Point |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| LEADER | Liraglutide | Reduced MACE and mortality signals in high-risk type 2 diabetes. |
| SUSTAIN 6 | Semaglutide | Reduced 3-point MACE in high-risk type 2 diabetes; retinopathy signal requires attention. |
| REWIND | Dulaglutide | Long follow-up and broader risk population; reduced MACE. |
| FLOW | Semaglutide | Established kidney outcome benefit in type 2 diabetes with CKD. |

## Clinical Takeaways

- **ASCVD/weight phenotype:** GLP-1 RA is especially useful when ASCVD risk and weight reduction are central.
- **Different from SGLT2:** For heart failure and CKD, SGLT2 inhibitors remain foundational; GLP-1 RA can be layered for ASCVD, weight, glycemia, and now semaglutide CKD evidence.
- **Agent-specific evidence matters:** Do not assume every GLP-1 RA has identical CVOT strength.
- **GI and gallbladder issues:** Tolerability and dose escalation often determine persistence.
