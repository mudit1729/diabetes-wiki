# FLOW

## Clinical Question
In type 2 diabetes with chronic kidney disease, does once-weekly semaglutide reduce kidney failure, major kidney decline, kidney death, or cardiovascular death?

## Why It Matters
FLOW is the key kidney-outcome trial for semaglutide in type 2 diabetes with CKD. It moves semaglutide from "GLP-1 RA with cardiovascular and weight benefit" toward a layered cardiorenal therapy alongside SGLT2 inhibitors, renin-angiotensin system blockade, and finerenone where appropriate.

## Clinical Takeaways

- **Kidney outcome benefit:** Semaglutide reduced clinically important kidney outcomes and cardiovascular death in type 2 diabetes with CKD.
- **Layered therapy:** FLOW should be interpreted with KDIGO/ADA-KDIGO frameworks, not as a replacement for SGLT2 inhibitors when SGLT2 therapy is indicated and tolerated.
- **High-risk CKD population:** Applicability is strongest for albuminuric CKD matching trial entry criteria.
- **Implementation:** Monitor GI tolerability, volume status, nutrition/weight loss, retinopathy history, and concomitant insulin/sulfonylurea hypoglycemia risk.
