# DCCT / EDIC

## Clinical Question
In type 1 diabetes, does intensive insulin therapy reduce long-term microvascular and cardiovascular complications compared with conventional therapy?

## Why It Matters
DCCT established intensive insulin therapy as the foundation for type 1 diabetes complication prevention. EDIC follow-up showed long-term persistence of benefit, supporting early durable control and modern technology use to achieve control with less hypoglycemia.

## Clinical Takeaways

- **Microvascular proof:** Intensive therapy reduced development/progression of retinopathy, nephropathy, and neuropathy.
- **Legacy effect:** Early glycemic separation carried long-term vascular benefit even after later A1C convergence.
- **Hypoglycemia is the tradeoff:** The original intensive approach increased severe hypoglycemia; CGM and automated insulin delivery aim to preserve benefit while reducing harm.
- **Applies beyond A1C:** Time in range, glucose variability, and hypoglycemia burden are now practical endpoints layered on the DCCT principle.
