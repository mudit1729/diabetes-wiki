# SUSTAIN 1-6

## Clinical Question
Across type 2 diabetes treatment contexts, does once-weekly subcutaneous semaglutide improve glycemic control, weight, and cardiovascular safety/outcomes?

## Program Map

| Trial | Comparator / Setting | Practical Point |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------|
| SUSTAIN 1 | Placebo monotherapy | Semaglutide lowered A1C and weight in drug-naive patients. |
| SUSTAIN 2 | Sitagliptin | Greater A1C and weight reduction than DPP-4 inhibitor therapy. |
| SUSTAIN 3 | Exenatide ER | Greater glycemic and weight effect than another weekly GLP-1 RA comparator. |
| SUSTAIN 4 | Insulin glargine | In insulin-naive patients on oral therapy, semaglutide improved A1C/weight with less hypoglycemia than basal insulin strategies. |
| SUSTAIN 5 | Add-on to basal insulin | Semaglutide added to basal insulin improved A1C and weight versus placebo. |
| SUSTAIN 6 | Placebo CVOT | In high cardiovascular-risk type 2 diabetes, semaglutide reduced 3-point MACE versus placebo. |

## Clinical Takeaways

- **A1C plus weight:** SUSTAIN 1-5 made semaglutide a high-efficacy glucose-lowering and weight-lowering option.
- **Cardiovascular signal:** SUSTAIN 6 shifted semaglutide from glucose drug to cardiorenal-metabolic drug class evidence.
- **Retinopathy caution:** Rapid A1C reduction in patients with pre-existing retinopathy requires eye history and follow-up.
- **Insulin sparing:** SUSTAIN 4/5 are useful when trying to avoid or reduce insulin-associated weight gain and hypoglycemia.
- **Access matters:** Cost and supply often determine whether evidence can be implemented.

## India / South Asia Notes

- Weekly dosing can help adherence, but out-of-pocket cost is a major barrier.
- GI adverse effects can collide with local meal patterns, fasting, and travel; dose escalation counseling matters.
- For patients already on sulfonylurea or insulin, proactively reduce hypoglycemia risk when adding semaglutide.
