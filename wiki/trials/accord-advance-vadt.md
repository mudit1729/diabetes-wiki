# ACCORD / ADVANCE / VADT

## Clinical Question
In patients with established type 2 diabetes and high cardiovascular risk, does intensive glucose lowering prevent macrovascular events safely?

## Program Map

| Trial | Population Signal | Practical Point |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|
| ACCORD | High-risk, established type 2 diabetes | Very intensive A1C targeting increased mortality and stopped the glucose arm early. |
| ADVANCE | Broad high-risk type 2 diabetes | Intensive control reduced combined major macro/microvascular events mainly through nephropathy, not macrovascular events. |
| VADT | Long-duration type 2 diabetes in veterans | No clear early macrovascular benefit from intensive control; hypoglycemia and duration matter. |

## Clinical Takeaways

- **Targets must be individualized:** These trials are the counterweight to simplistic "lower is always better" diabetes care.
- **Avoid rapid high-burden intensification:** Frailty, long diabetes duration, ASCVD, CKD, hypoglycemia, and polypharmacy should soften goals.
- **Microvascular benefit persists:** ADVANCE supports kidney/microvascular benefit, but not at any cost.
- **Drug choice changed since 2008:** Modern GLP-1 RA and SGLT2 inhibitor outcome benefits are not the same question as forcing A1C below 6% with older regimens.
