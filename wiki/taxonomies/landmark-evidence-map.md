# Landmark Diabetes Evidence Map

Doctor-facing map of the practice-changing diabetes evidence. These summaries are clinically oriented seed notes from the multi-agent review pass; full OCR-ingested pages should still be added through [[wiki/taxonomies/evidence-ingestion-queue|Evidence Ingestion Queue]] when an open-access PDF is available.

## Glycemic Foundations

| Paper / Program | Clinical question | Main finding | Practice implication | Links |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DCCT / EDIC | Does near-normal glycemia prevent complications in type 1 diabetes, and do benefits persist? | DCCT reduced retinopathy, nephropathy, and neuropathy; EDIC showed durable legacy benefit, including later CVD benefit. | In type 1 diabetes, pursue early intensive glycemic management when safely achievable. | [[wiki/trials/dcct-edic|Wiki]] · [DCCT PMID 8366922](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/8366922/) · [EDIC CVD PMID 16371630](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16371630/) |
| UKPDS 33 | Does intensive glucose control reduce complications in newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes? | Lower A1C reduced diabetes-related endpoints and microvascular disease. | Establishes early glycemic control as microvascular prevention in type 2 diabetes. | [[wiki/trials/ukpds|Wiki]] · [PMID 9742976](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9742976/) |
| UKPDS 34 | Is metformin beneficial in overweight newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes? | Metformin reduced diabetes-related endpoints, diabetes-related death, and all-cause mortality. | Supports metformin as foundational first-line therapy when tolerated and renal function permits. | [[wiki/medications/metformin|Metformin]] · [PMID 9742977](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9742977/) |
| UKPDS 80 | Do early intensive-control benefits persist after trial end? | Persistent microvascular benefit and later MI/mortality reductions, especially with metformin in overweight patients. | Early durable glycemic control can yield long-term benefit. | [[wiki/trials/ukpds|Wiki]] · [PMID 18784090](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18784090/) |
| ACCORD | Does targeting A1C below 6.0% reduce CV events in high-risk type 2 diabetes? | Intensive arm had higher mortality and no significant primary CV benefit. | Avoid aggressive near-normal targets in high-risk longstanding diabetes. | [[wiki/trials/accord-advance-vadt|Wiki]] · [PMID 18539917](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18539917/) |
| ADVANCE | Does gradual intensive glucose lowering reduce vascular outcomes? | Reduced combined micro/macrovascular outcome mainly through nephropathy; no clear macrovascular or mortality benefit. | Intensive control can reduce renal microvascular outcomes when gradual and safe. | [[wiki/trials/accord-advance-vadt|Wiki]] · [PMID 18539916](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18539916/) |
| VADT | Does intensive control reduce vascular events in longstanding poorly controlled diabetes? | No significant reduction in major CV events or death during main trial; more hypoglycemia. | Individualize targets in advanced disease and prioritize hypoglycemia prevention. | [[wiki/algorithms/hypoglycemia-risk|Hypoglycemia risk]] · [PMID 19092145](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19092145/) |
| DPP | Can lifestyle or metformin prevent diabetes in high-risk adults? | Diabetes incidence reduced 58% with lifestyle and 31% with metformin versus placebo. | Intensive lifestyle is first-line prevention; metformin is evidence-based for selected high-risk patients. | [[wiki/medications/metformin|Metformin]] · [PMID 11832527](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11832527/) |
| GRADE | Which common add-on to metformin best maintains glycemia? | Glargine and liraglutide maintained A1C below 7% longer than glimepiride and sitagliptin. | Add-on choice should weigh durability, weight, hypoglycemia, CV/CKD needs, cost, and access. | [[wiki/scenarios/new-insulin-start-primary-care|Insulin start]] · [Glycemic PMID 36129996](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36129996/) · [Outcomes PMID 36129997](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36129997/) |

## GLP-1 / Incretin Outcomes

| Trial / Program | Clinical question | Main finding | Practice implication | Links |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| LEADER | Does liraglutide reduce CV events in high-risk type 2 diabetes? | Reduced 3-point MACE, CV death, and all-cause death. | GLP-1 RA option when ASCVD/high CV risk is prominent. | [[wiki/trials/glp1-cvot-program|GLP-1 CVOT Program]] · [PMID 27295427](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27295427/) |
| SUSTAIN 1-6 | Does semaglutide improve glycemia/weight and CV outcomes? | Strong A1C/weight lowering; SUSTAIN-6 reduced MACE with nephropathy signal and retinopathy complication signal. | Potent glycemic/weight therapy; assess retinopathy and avoid overly rapid A1C drops in advanced retinopathy. | [[wiki/trials/sustain-program|SUSTAIN Program]] · [[wiki/medications/semaglutide|Semaglutide]] · [SUSTAIN-6 PMID 27633186](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27633186/) |
| REWIND | Does dulaglutide reduce MACE in a broader population? | Reduced 3-point MACE over median 5.4 years, including many without prior CVD. | Strong GLP-1 RA evidence for primary plus secondary CV prevention. | [[wiki/trials/glp1-cvot-program|GLP-1 CVOT Program]] · [PMID 31189511](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31189511/) |
| FLOW | Does semaglutide improve hard kidney outcomes in type 2 diabetes with CKD? | Reduced kidney/CV-death composite; lower MACE and all-cause death signals. | Supports semaglutide as add-on cardiorenal therapy in type 2 diabetes with CKD. | [[wiki/trials/flow|FLOW]] · [[wiki/algorithms/type-2-diabetes-with-ckd|CKD algorithm]] · [PMID 38785209](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38785209/) |
| SURPASS-CVOT | Is tirzepatide CV-safe/effective versus dulaglutide in type 2 diabetes with ASCVD? | Tirzepatide was noninferior but not statistically superior for 3-point MACE versus dulaglutide; greater A1C/weight effects. | Confirms CV safety against active GLP-1 RA comparator; selection hinges on weight/A1C goals, tolerability, cost, and evolving kidney data. | [[wiki/medications/tirzepatide|Tirzepatide]] · [PMID 41406444](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41406444/) · [DOI 10.1056/NEJMoa2505928](https://doi.org/10.1056/NEJMoa2505928) |

## SGLT2 / Kidney / Heart Failure

| Trial | Clinical question | Main finding | Practice implication | Links |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| EMPA-REG OUTCOME | Does empagliflozin improve CV outcomes in type 2 diabetes with established ASCVD? | Reduced MACE, CV death, HF hospitalization, and all-cause death. | High-value SGLT2 inhibitor when HF hospitalization/CV death risk is prominent. | [[wiki/medications/empagliflozin|Empagliflozin]] · [PMID 26378978](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26378978/) |
| CANVAS Program | Does canagliflozin reduce CV and renal events? | Reduced MACE with HF and renal benefit signals; amputation signal requires clinical context. | SGLT2 benefit with foot/PAD/amputation risk assessment when selecting canagliflozin. | [[wiki/trials/sglt2-cvot-program|SGLT2 CVOT Program]] · [PMID 28605608](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28605608/) |
| DECLARE-TIMI 58 | Does dapagliflozin improve outcomes in ASCVD or multiple-risk-factor type 2 diabetes? | MACE neutral for superiority; CV death/HF hospitalization reduced, driven by less HF hospitalization; renal events reduced. | Dapagliflozin's strongest CVOT signal is HF/renal prevention in broad type 2 diabetes populations. | [[wiki/medications/dapagliflozin|Dapagliflozin]] · [PMID 30415602](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30415602/) |
| CREDENCE | Does canagliflozin slow diabetic kidney disease progression? | Reduced renal/CV-death composite, ESKD, MACE, and HF hospitalization. | Established SGLT2 inhibitors as disease-modifying therapy for albuminuric diabetic CKD. | [[wiki/algorithms/type-2-diabetes-with-ckd|CKD algorithm]] · [PMID 30990260](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30990260/) |
| DAPA-CKD | Does dapagliflozin improve CKD outcomes with or without diabetes? | Reduced kidney/CV-death composite; benefits consistent regardless of diabetes. | SGLT2 inhibitors are kidney-protective CKD drugs, not only glucose-lowering drugs. | [[wiki/medications/dapagliflozin|Dapagliflozin]] · [PMID 32970396](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32970396/) |
| EMPA-KIDNEY | Does empagliflozin help broader CKD? | Reduced kidney progression/CV death in CKD with and without diabetes. | Expands SGLT2 kidney protection to broader CKD, including lower eGFR and many without diabetes. | [[wiki/medications/empagliflozin|Empagliflozin]] · [PMID 36331190](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36331190/) |

## Residual Albuminuric CKD Risk

| Trial | Clinical question | Main finding | Practice implication | Links |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FIDELIO-DKD | Does finerenone reduce residual kidney/CV risk despite ACEi/ARB? | Reduced kidney composite and CV composite; more hyperkalemia discontinuation. | Add finerenone for persistent albuminuric diabetic CKD risk with potassium/eGFR monitoring. | [[wiki/medications/finerenone|Finerenone]] · [PMID 33264825](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33264825/) |
| FIGARO-DKD | Does finerenone reduce CV events across earlier CKD spectrum? | Reduced CV composite, mainly fewer HF hospitalizations; kidney benefit directionally favorable. | Supports finerenone for CV/HF residual risk in albuminuric type 2 diabetes CKD. | [[wiki/medications/finerenone|Finerenone]] · [PMID 34449181](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34449181/) |

## Insulin, Technology, Pregnancy, Inpatient

| Paper / Program | Clinical question | Main finding | Practice implication | Links |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ORIGIN | Is early basal insulin glargine CV-safe and outcome-improving? | CV and cancer neutral; reduced progression to diabetes in those without diabetes, but increased hypoglycemia and weight. | Basal insulin is CV-safe but not a CV-risk-reduction drug. | [[wiki/medications/basal-insulin|Basal insulin]] · [PMID 22686416](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/22686416/) |
| DEVOTE | Is insulin degludec CV-safe versus glargine U100 and safer for hypoglycemia? | Noninferior for MACE; severe and nocturnal severe hypoglycemia were lower with degludec. | Choose degludec when severe/nocturnal hypoglycemia risk is a priority. | [[wiki/algorithms/hypoglycemia-risk|Hypoglycemia risk]] · [PMID 28605603](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28605603/) |
| HAPO | Is maternal glycemia below overt diabetes thresholds associated with adverse outcomes? | Continuous graded association with birthweight, cord C-peptide, neonatal hypoglycemia, cesarean delivery, and related outcomes. | Supports modern lower-threshold gestational diabetes diagnostic frameworks. | [[wiki/taxonomies/research-map|Pregnancy domain]] · [PMID 18463375](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18463375/) |
| WISDM / CGM evidence | Does CGM improve glycemia and reduce hypoglycemia in key populations? | WISDM reduced hypoglycemia in older adults with type 1 diabetes; DIAMOND/GOLD improved A1C; CONCEPTT improved pregnancy/neonatal outcomes. | CGM is standard-of-care for type 1 diabetes and high hypoglycemia-risk scenarios. | [[wiki/algorithms/hypoglycemia-risk|Hypoglycemia risk]] · [WISDM PMID 32543682](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32543682/) · [DIAMOND PMID 28118453](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28118453/) · [GOLD PMID 28118454](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28118454/) · [CONCEPTT PMID 28923465](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28923465/) |
| NICE-SUGAR | Should ICU patients receive intensive normoglycemia-targeted insulin therapy? | Intensive control increased 90-day mortality and severe hypoglycemia. | Avoid ICU normoglycemia targets; use moderate inpatient targets for most critically ill patients. | [[wiki/taxonomies/research-map|Acute/inpatient domain]] · [PMID 19318384](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19318384/) |
