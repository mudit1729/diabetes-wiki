# Type 2 Diabetes With Obesity and ASCVD

## Typical Patient

Type 2 diabetes with prior MI, stroke, PAD, or coronary revascularization; BMI elevated; A1C above individualized goal; no dominant heart failure syndrome.

## First Visit Priorities

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| ASCVD risk | Confirm statin intensity, BP plan, smoking status, antiplatelet indication. |
| Weight/A1C therapy | Prefer GLP-1 RA with CVOT benefit or GIP/GLP-1 therapy when weight effect dominates and outcome data/label fit. |
| CKD/HF overlap | If albuminuria, low eGFR, or HF is present, prioritize SGLT2 inhibitor early. |
| Hypoglycemia | Reduce sulfonylurea/insulin as incretin therapy improves glucose. |
| Access | Discuss affordability and injection acceptance before writing a plan that will not be filled. |

## Practical Medication Direction

1. Use [[wiki/medications/semaglutide|semaglutide]] when ASCVD/weight/A1C benefit is the main priority and label/tolerability fit.
2. Consider [[wiki/medications/tirzepatide|tirzepatide]] when obesity and glycemic efficacy are dominant, while checking current outcome evidence and formulary.
3. Add [[wiki/medications/empagliflozin|empagliflozin]] or [[wiki/medications/dapagliflozin|dapagliflozin]] if CKD or heart failure overlaps.
4. Continue [[wiki/medications/metformin|metformin]] if tolerated and not contraindicated.
5. De-intensify [[wiki/medications/sulfonylureas|sulfonylureas]] or insulin when hypoglycemia risk rises.

## Avoid

- Calling this "just weight loss" when ASCVD risk reduction is part of the treatment logic.
- Missing retinopathy history before rapid A1C lowering.
- Escalating insulin before trying weight-friendly options when clinically feasible.
