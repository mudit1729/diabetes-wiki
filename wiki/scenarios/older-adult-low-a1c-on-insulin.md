# Older Adult With A1C 6.4% on Insulin

## Typical Patient

Older adult with type 2 diabetes, A1C below or near normal, using basal or basal-bolus insulin, with comorbidity, CKD, falls, cognitive issues, or limited support.

## First Visit Priorities

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Reframe goal | Decide whether current A1C is unnecessarily tight. |
| Find hidden lows | Ask about nocturnal symptoms, skipped meals, falls, confusion, and CGM/SMBG lows. |
| Simplify | Reduce insulin burden before adding complexity. |
| Protect outcomes | Keep cardiorenal drugs if appropriate and safe. |
| Support | Check caregiver involvement, vision/manual ability, and affordability. |

## Practical Medication Direction

1. Reduce [[wiki/medications/basal-insulin|basal insulin]] if fasting or overnight lows are present.
2. Stop prandial insulin first if meals are inconsistent and A1C is very low.
3. Stop [[wiki/medications/sulfonylureas|sulfonylurea]] if still present.
4. Continue low-risk, high-value therapies such as [[wiki/medications/metformin|metformin]] if safe.
5. Use CGM when feasible to confirm de-intensification is safe.

## Avoid

- Treating A1C 6.4% as automatically excellent in a high-risk older adult.
- Continuing complex insulin regimens after falls or severe lows.
- Stopping all diabetes therapy when the right move is targeted simplification.
