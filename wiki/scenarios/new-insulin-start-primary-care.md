# New Insulin Start in Primary Care

## Typical Patient

Type 2 diabetes with persistent symptomatic hyperglycemia, very high glucose, or A1C well above goal despite oral/non-insulin therapy; no clear DKA/HHS requiring acute care.

## First Visit Priorities

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Rule out emergency | Check symptoms, ketones when indicated, dehydration, infection, weight loss, mental status. |
| Confirm insulin need | Consider GLP-1 RA before insulin if not symptomatic/catabolic and access allows. |
| Start simple | Basal insulin with a written titration plan is safer than complex regimens for most starts. |
| Teach safety | Hypoglycemia recognition, treatment, injection technique, storage, sharps, sick-day plan. |
| Follow closely | Early phone/portal follow-up prevents both inertia and hypoglycemia. |

## Practical Medication Direction

1. Continue [[wiki/medications/metformin|metformin]] if safe.
2. Continue SGLT2 inhibitor or GLP-1 RA when indicated and safe; do not stop outcome drugs reflexively.
3. Start [[wiki/medications/basal-insulin|basal insulin]] with fasting glucose titration.
4. Reduce or stop [[wiki/medications/sulfonylureas|sulfonylurea]] as insulin begins working.
5. If fasting glucose reaches goal but A1C remains high, address post-prandial glucose rather than endlessly increasing basal insulin.

## Minimum Teaching Checklist

- How many units, what time, what pen/vial, where to inject.
- What fasting glucose target to use.
- How and when to titrate.
- What number is "too low."
- What to do for hypoglycemia.
- When to call the clinic.
